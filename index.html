How is this calculated?
Instead of comparing carbon alone, we express each activity as a fraction of its annual planetary boundary — the safe operating limit for humanity (Rockström et al.).

Per glass of almond milk (~17g almonds):
Carbon: 0.35 kg CO₂e ÷ 1,500 kg/yr budget = 0.023%
Water: 74 L (at ~4,300 L/kg almonds) ÷ 500,000 L/yr budget = 0.015%
Combined: 0.038%

Per glass of cow's milk (~240ml):
Carbon: 0.76 kg CO₂e ÷ 1,500 kg/yr budget = 0.051%
Water: 150 L ÷ 500,000 L/yr budget = 0.030%
Combined: 0.081%

Per mile of commercial flight:
Carbon: 0.25 kg CO₂e ÷ 1,500 kg/yr budget = 0.017%
Water: 0.5 L ÷ 500,000 L/yr budget = ≈ 0%
Combined: 0.017%

Exchange rates:
Almond milk: 0.017 ÷ 0.038 = 0.44 glasses per mile
Cow's milk: 0.017 ÷ 0.081 = 0.21 glasses per mile

Cow's milk has ~2.1× the boundary impact per glass compared to almond milk — higher on both carbon (2.2×) and water (2×). Switching to cow's milk would make things worse, not better.

Boundary budgets: ~1,500 kg CO₂e/person/year (1.5°C pathway); ~500,000 L consumptive freshwater/person/year (Rockström et al., 2009). Cow's milk: ~3.2 kg CO₂e/L, ~628 L water/L (Poore & Nemecek, 2018).
