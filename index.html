Combined carbon + water planetary boundary impact
How is this calculated?
Instead of comparing carbon alone, we express each activity as a fraction of its annual planetary boundary — the safe operating limit for humanity (Rockström et al.).

Per glass of almond milk:
Carbon: 0.35 kg ÷ 1,500 kg budget = 0.023%
Water: 74 L ÷ 500,000 L budget = 0.015%
Combined: 0.038%

Per mile of flight:
Carbon: 0.25 kg ÷ 1,500 kg budget = 0.017%
Water: 0.5 L ÷ 500,000 L budget = ≈ 0%
Combined: 0.017%

Ratio: 0.017 ÷ 0.038 = 0.44 glasses per mile.
This means each flight mile costs about 0.44 glasses of almond milk in total planetary boundary impact — accounting for both the carbon and the water almonds require.
Assumptions: ~0.35 kg CO₂e per glass of almond milk; ~0.25 kg CO₂e per passenger-mile of flight; ~74 liters of water per glass of almond milk (~17g almonds at ~4,300 liters/kg); ~0.5 liters of water per passenger-mile of flight (minimal). Planetary boundary budgets: ~1,500 kg CO₂e/person/year (1.5°C pathway), ~500,000 liters consumptive freshwater/person/year (Rockström). Combined rate: each flight mile uses 0.017% of combined boundaries vs. 0.038% per glass, yielding 1 mile ≈ 0.44 glasses.
